The Lion and the Jewel

"The Lion and the Jewel" is a play by Nigerian Nobel laureate Wole Soyinka, first performed in 1962. The story revolves around the village of Ilujinle, where the traditional way of life is disrupted by the arrival of the British.

Key Features and Interesting Facts:

  • Explores themes of cultural identity, colonialism, and tradition vs. modernity.
  • Written by Wole Soyinka, Nigeria's first Nobel Prize winner (1986).
  • Considered a classic of Nigerian literature and African drama.

Author: Wole Soyinka
